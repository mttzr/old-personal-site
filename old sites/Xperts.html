What does an XPERT do?

XPERT sessions take around 30 minutes, and your Partner app guides you through every step.

  • Meet excited new drivers
  • Perform a quick quality check
  • Help new drivers get organized
  • Cover Uber basics and share tips
Next

Meet excited new drivers

Go online as usual (you'll see the same screen as a normal ride request)

The app will automatically switch to XPERT mode after you're approved and a new driver is nearby.

Once you accept an XPERT request, head to their location and park nearby to meet your new driver.

FYI: New drivers just signed up and are counting on your help. They should be waiting for you next to their car with their vehicle documents.

Next

Perform a quality check

Now, the app will guide you through a vehicle checklist to make sure your new driver's car is safe and ready to go.

PRO TIP: Safety and quality are important, so complete the checklist as accurately as possible. You can always edit your answers on the main vehicle checklist screen.

Exterior Check

First, please check the outside of the car to make sure it's in good condition. We'll ask you a series of step-by-step questions to guide you.(e.g. Check the windshield. Is it free of cracks or scratches?)

You'll be checking:

  • Headlights
  • Turn indicators
  • Windows
  • Windshield/Wipers
  • Taillights
  • Tires
  • Side Mirrors
  • Brake lights
  • Bumpers
  • Doors

Checking tires

IMPORTANT: Tires can be tricky, so you'll want to make sure all 4 tires are condition and not worn out, with at least 2/32” of tread depth.
You can use the penny test to check tread depth. Stick a penny down into a groove of the tire. If part of Lincoln’s head is covered, the tread is deep enough.

Interior Check

The last half of your vehicle checklist will be completed from the passenger's seat. Here, you'll ask the new driver to take a quick 10-minute test drive. Have them drive around the area so you can see how their car moves on the road, and don't forget to buckle up!

On your test drive you'll be checking:

  • Seats
  • Brakes
  • Speedometer
  • Rearview mirror
  • Emergency brake
  • Wheel and horn
Next

Help drivers get organized

Once your vehicle checklist and test drive are complete, we only need a few quick things to get them started!

Next up:

  • Take photos of important vehicle-related documents
  • Snap the new driver’s profile photo
  • Help them set up the Partner app

Photograph documents

Using the app, take clear photos of the new driver’s license, registration, and proof of insurance.

Make sure the entire document is in photo, otherwise it may take longer to process their application.

Take pictures using a solid background like the dashboard or the hood of their car.

Missing any documents? No problem. Just skip that section and ask new drivers to email XXXX@uber.com with missing docs once they have them.

Example documents

Take a profile photo

Your new driver needs a friendly new photo for their profile account, so riders can see who they are looking for when requesting rides.

Ask your driver to face the camera and smile, preferably in daylight (no hats or sunglasses, please).

Need to retake? Just tap the screen.

Set up the Uber Partner App

Almost done! Now, just help set up the Partner app for your new driver.

First, ask your new driver if they already downloaded the app. If they did, ask them to open it and show you.

If they don’t have it, please follow the steps on your screen to help them get the app.

Cover Uber basics and share tips

Last step! New drivers are excited and naturally curious, so they’ll have a lot of questions about driving. Now’s the time to answer questions and give your honest advice.

Recommended Topics:

  • How ride requests work
  • Your reasons for drivign with Uber
  • How weekly payments work
  • How to get support from Uber
  • Your favorite tips for great ratings

PRO TIP: Get a question you don’t know the answer to? New drivers can always contact Uber support for help.

Finishing an Xpert trip

Once you’ve answered all their questions, it’s time to say goodbye and get back to your riders.

Next steps for a new driver:

  • Let them know Uber has received their application and that they’ll be in touch within a few days.

Next steps for a you:
